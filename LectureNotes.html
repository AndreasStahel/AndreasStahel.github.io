Numerical Methods for the Master of Biomedical Engineering

This is a joint Master of the University of Bern and the Bern University of Applied Sciences, Engineering and Information Technology

The lecture notes used in this class are available at NumMethods.pdf.

Topics:
  • A few model problems to be examined: static and dynamic heat equation, deformation of strings and beams.
  • Matrix computations: floating point operations, vector and matrix norms, condition number, LR factorization, Cholesky, sparse matrix, iterative solvers, conjugate gradient, preconditioner.
  • Numerical tools: nonlinear equations, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, numerical integration, ordinary differential equations, linear and nonlinear regression, curve fitting.
  • Finite difference methods: approximation, consistency, stability, convergence, boundary value problems, initial boundary value problems.
  • Calculus of variations: Euler-Lagrange, quadratic functionals, Hamilton's principle.
  • Elasticity: stress, strain, invariants, failure modes, tensors, Hooke's law, elastic energy, nonlinear material laws, plane strain, plane stress, from minimization to PDE.
